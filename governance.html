Formation & Purpose

Q‑BOND Network operates through DeSCI‑Q DAO LLC, a Wyoming decentralized autonomous organization limited liability company. The DAO coordinates governance of the open‑source Q‑BOND protocol, which supports scientific research through community‑led scoring and digital collectible issuance. Importantly, the DAO does not issue financial instruments or investment products and has no centralized management.

Legal Framework

The DAO is a member‑managed organization governed by smart contracts deployed on multiple chains. The primary chain is QANX, with secondary deployments planned on Arbitrum and ZKSync. Governance relies entirely on autonomous software and community voting rather than human or corporate managers.

Membership

Any individual who holds a QIE identity, participates in governance or interacts with the protocol is considered a member. There are no restrictions based on location, wallet size or status. Members may submit proposals, vote and contribute to scoring and protocol evolution.

Liability & Risk

Members are not personally liable for the debts or obligations of the DAO. The DAO itself is not liable for loss of digital assets, smart contract bugs, project outcomes or governance decisions. Participation is entirely at the user’s own risk, reflecting the experimental nature of decentralized research and finance.

Purpose Limitation

The DAO operates exclusively to support decentralized research scoring, fund scientific progress via digital collectibles and enable open, identity‑based contribution mechanisms. Q‑BONDs are not investments and do not entitle holders to profits, equity or guaranteed yield.

Amendments

Changes to this operating agreement may be proposed and passed by DAO vote. All amendments must be implemented through on‑chain governance and recorded in DAO records.
